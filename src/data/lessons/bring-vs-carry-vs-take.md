# Bring vs Carry vs Take

**`Bring`**, **`Carry`**, and **`Take`** can all involve moving something, but they focus on **different ideas**:

| Word      | Main Idea                                      | Focus                                           |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Bring** | Move something **toward** a person or place    | Direction **toward here / toward destination**  |
| **Take**  | Move something **away** from a person or place | Direction **away from here / to another place** |
| **Carry** | Hold and transport something physically        | The **act of holding or transporting**          |

## Quick Comparison Table

| Sentence                            | Why?                           |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| *Bring your notebook to class.*     | Moving toward class            |
| *Take your notebook home.*          | Moving away to another place   |
| *Carry your notebook carefully.*    | Focus on holding it physically |
| *Can you bring me some water?*      | Toward speaker                 |
| *He took the package downstairs.*   | Away from current place        |
| *She carried the baby for an hour.* | Physical transport             |

## Easy rule

```text
Bring → toward
Take → away
Carry → physically transport
```

A useful trick:

**"Bring here → Take there → Carry physically."**

## Common Comparison

Situation: You are at home talking to your friend at the office.

* *I'll **bring** the documents to your office.*
  → Movement toward your friend.

* *I'll **take** the documents to the office.*
  → Movement away from your current location.

* *I'll **carry** the documents in my backpack.*
  → Focus on physically transporting them.

---

## 1. Bring = Move something toward a place or person

Use **bring** when something moves **toward the speaker or toward a destination being discussed**.

**Structure:**

```text
bring + object (+ to + place/person)
```

Examples:

* *Can you bring your laptop to the meeting?*
  → ¿Puedes traer tu portátil a la reunión?

* *She brought coffee for everyone.*
  → Ella trajo café para todos.

### When to use it:

Imagine the movement is **coming toward where someone is**.

✅ *Bring your passport to the airport.*
❌ *Take your passport to me.* (unnatural if you're the destination)

---

## 2. Take = Move something away to another place

Use **take** when something goes **from one place to another**, usually **away from the current location**.

**Structure:**

```text
take + object (+ to + place)
```

Examples:

* *I need to take these documents to the office.*
  → Necesito llevar estos documentos a la oficina.

* *She took her dog to the park.*
  → Ella llevó a su perro al parque.

### When to use it:

Think of movement **away from where you are now**.

If you're at home:

* *I will take my laptop to work.* ✅
* *I will bring my laptop to work.* ❌ (unless talking from the perspective of work)

---

## 3. Carry = Hold and transport physically

Use **carry** when emphasizing **physically holding or supporting something while moving**.

**Structure:**

```text
carry + object
```

Examples:

* *He carried a heavy box upstairs.*
  → Él cargó una caja pesada hacia arriba.

* *She carries her phone everywhere.*
  → Ella lleva su teléfono a todas partes.

### When to use it:

Focus on **how something is moved**, not the destination.

Examples:

* *The backpack is too heavy to carry.*
  → La mochila es demasiado pesada para cargarla.

* *Can you carry these bags?*
  → ¿Puedes cargar estas bolsas?
